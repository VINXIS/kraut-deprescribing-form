Source Bottom line
Fracture prevention
Vitamin D and Calcium Tools for Practice #374 (adults, 2024)
  • No evidence of fracture reduction with Vitamin D. Vitamin D and calcium might reduce fractures and hip fractures in women in LTC from 11.5% to 10.9 and 1.8% to 1.6%, might increase risk of renal calculi from 2.1 to 2.5%.
Bisphosphonate STOPPFrail (frail older adults, 2017 and 2020)
  • Challenging to administer, adverse effects, and clinical benefit may continue after discontinuing
Cardiovascular
Statin Systematic review and meta-analysis (frail older adults, 2024)
  • No evidence of reduced mortality (primary prevention)
Acetylsalicylic acid Canadian Cardiovascular Society guidelines (adults, 2024)
  • No evidence of reduced cardiovascular events (primary prevention)
Antihypertensives Narrative review (frail older adults, 2022)
  • No evidence of reduced cardiovascular events, may be harmful (falls, orthostatic hypotension, cognitive decline)
Vitamins
Multivitamin Tools for practice #87 (adults, 2016)
  • No evidence routine use reduces mortality, cardiovascular disease, and/or cancer
B12 Systematic review (adults, 2021)
  • No evidence of improvement in cognitive function, depressive symptoms, or fatigue when not deficient
Bladder
BPH treatment and Overactive Bladder International Consultation on Incontinence (frail older adults, 2020)
  • Do not treat frail older adults who are incontinent with BPH and OAB medications.
Diabetes
Antihyperglycemic1
(not including insulin)		 STOPPFrail (frail older adults, 2020)
  • Avoid HbA1C target <7.5% (average 10.1 mmol/L glucose) as it is associated with net harm.
Other
Acetaminophen Usage in LTC (frail older adults, 2025)
  • No evidence of improvement in pain
Melatonin CADTH review (adults, 2022)
  • No evidence it improves any aspect of sleep
Iron Tools for practice (older adults, 2015)
  • Select low-dose iron as it has similar efficacy and fewer adverse effects.
